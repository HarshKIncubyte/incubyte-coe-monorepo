# Giving and Receiving Feedback

## Key Notes

### Giving Feedback

* Give feedback with the intention of helping the other person improve, not to prove a point.
* Check your emotional state before giving feedback. Avoid giving feedback when angry or frustrated.
* Focus on observable behaviour and its impact. Avoid assumptions about intentions or motives.
* Ask for permission before giving feedback whenever possible.
* Give feedback privately and in a timely manner.
* Use the SBIN framework:

  * **Situation:** Describe the context.
  * **Behavior:** Explain what you observed.
  * **Impact:** Describe the effect.
  * **Next:** Discuss the way forward.
* Feedback should be a two-way conversation. Invite feedback in return.

### Receiving Feedback

* Listen carefully without interrupting.
* Don't defend, justify, or explain your actions immediately.
* Don't lead the conversation or put words into the other person's mouth.
* Ask only clarifying questions to better understand the feedback.
* Don't make promises on the spot. Take time to reflect before deciding what to change.
* Thank the person for taking the time to share their feedback.
* Reflect on the feedback later and decide what actions to take.
